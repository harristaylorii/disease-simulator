Welcome to your very own Disease Spread Simulator! Inspired by the works of Harry Stevens from the Washington Post and Grant Sanderson on YouTube, I decided to create a program that allows users to explore disease spread in an interactive environment. From social distancing to infection rate, you can simulate a variety of disease events from the common cold (Recovery Time = Fast, Social Distancing = None, Infection Rate = 50%) to the Bubonic Plague (Recovery Time = Medium, Social Distancing = Light, Movement Speed = Slow, Infection Rate = 90%, Fatality Rate = 60%) to our own COVID-19 pandemic (Recovery Time = Slow, Social Distance = Extreme, Infection Rate= 75%, Fatality Rate = 2%). Let me know if you enjoy the simulation or find a way to improve it!

Here are some descriptions for each of the variables you can play with:

  • Number of individuals
    - These are all the dots seen on the screen. Be careful running the simulation with more than 1000 individuals. It may slow your browser. You'll notice that when you have less than 300 individuals, they bounce off each other to better represent the infection rate. If you more than 300 individuals, they just pass through each other, because repeatedly bouncing off deceased individuals in a community is just a little too morbid...
  • Recovery Time
    - How long an individual stays "sick." A slow recovery time means they are sick longer.
  • Social Distancing
    - These measures force a certain amount of individuals to "shelter in place."
  • Quarantine
    - Produces an impermeable barrier that prevents individuals from coming into contact with one another. The quarantine works best with fewer individuals.
  • Movement Speed
    - How quickly individuals are moving around.
  • Infection Rate
    - The probability that a healthy individual becomes ill after coming in contact with a sick individual.
  • Fatality Rate
    - The probability that an individual dies from the disease. Deceased individuals are represented by black dots.
